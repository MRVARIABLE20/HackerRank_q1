1. POLICY PURPOSE
-----------------
This policy defines the rules for retaining and deleting personal data in accordance
with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) Articles 5(1)(e), 13, and 17.
All data controllers and processors within the company must comply.

2. LEGAL BASIS FOR RETENTION
-----------------------------
Personal data may only be retained where one of the following lawful bases applies:
  a) Consent (Art. 6(1)(a))     — explicit opt-in obtained; withdrawal honoured within 30 days
  b) Contract (Art. 6(1)(b))    — data necessary to fulfil a contract with the data subject
  c) Legal obligation (6(1)(c)) — statutory or regulatory requirement to retain
  d) Legitimate interest (6(1)(f)) — documented LIA completed; override test passed

3. RETENTION SCHEDULES
-----------------------
Data Category              | Retention Period  | Basis          | Deletion Method
---------------------------|-------------------|----------------|------------------
Customer PII (active)      | Duration + 3 yrs  | Contract       | Secure wipe
Customer PII (churned)     | 3 years from churn| Legitimate int.| Secure wipe
Employee records           | 7 years           | Legal (HMRC)   | Shred + wipe
Financial transaction logs | 7 years           | Legal (AML)    | Secure wipe
Marketing contact data     | 2 years           | Consent        | Anonymise
Support tickets (PII)      | 2 years           | Contract       | Anonymise
Application logs (PII)     | 90 days           | Legitimate int.| Auto-purge
Security audit logs        | 12 months         | Legitimate int.| Secure wipe
Website analytics (anon.)  | 26 months         | Legitimate int.| Aggregation
Backup copies (encrypted)  | +30 days lag      | Legal/Contract | Purge on schedule

4. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS
-----------------------
Right to Erasure (Art. 17): Requests fulfilled within 30 days unless legal hold applies.
Right of Access (Art. 15):  Subject Access Requests fulfilled within 30 days.
Right to Rectification:     Corrections processed within 72 hours.
Right to Portability:       Data exported in JSON/CSV format on request.

5. CROSS-BORDER TRANSFERS
--------------------------
Personal data may only be transferred outside the EEA where:
  - Adequacy decision exists (Art. 45), OR
  - Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) are in place (Art. 46), OR
  - Binding Corporate Rules approved by lead DPA

Current approved transfer mechanisms:
  - US: AWS us-east-1 — SCCs (2021 version) signed with AWS Ireland
  - UK: UK GDPR adequacy confirmed; same SCCs applied

6. BREACH NOTIFICATION
-----------------------
Suspected breaches must be reported to DPO within 2 hours of discovery.
DPO reports to ICO within 72 hours if risk to data subjects is likely.
Affected data subjects notified without undue delay if high risk.
All breach events logged in the incident register (ref. INC-GDPR-*).
